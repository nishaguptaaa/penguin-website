Hello! My name is Nisha, and I am passionate about penguins. I find these birds super cute and very interesting to learn about! Penguins are flightless birds that have adapted to life in the water. There are around 18 known species. Penguins are found mostly in the Southern Hemisphere (only one species is found north of the equator!), these adorable creatures are known for their waddle, interesting social behavior, and incredible swimming skills. They camouflage in the water with their black and white plumage and are known to hunt for fish, squid, and krill. They are known to be strong swimmers rather than to fly. They can use their flippers to move themselves up to 15 miles per hour in the ocean and are able to retain heat through their dense feathers. While they are known to waddle on land, they can slide on their bellies in way referred to as "toboggoning", which helps them travel over ice quickly. The largest penguin species, Emperor penguins, can hold their breath for more than 20 minutes! Most penguins are very social creatures, and they are known to form large breeding colonies which can reach thousands in population. They use distinct vocalizations to communicate amongst each other. This website is dedicated to learning more about these amazing animals!
